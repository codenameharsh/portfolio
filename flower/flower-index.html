Bloom & Glow: Floral Art

Case Study | Mini Floral Art Collection

Duration: 2 days

Project Overview

  • Client: Teller’s Books (Fictional)
  • Project Type: Hero Illustration + Supporting Patterns
  • Role: Illustrator & Surface Pattern Designer
  • Deliverables: 1 Hero Illustration, 1 Secondary Floral Pattern, 1 Blender Pattern

Challenge Overview

Teller’s Books, a fictional stationery and gift product manufacturer, envisioned a spring-themed “indie” artist collection centered around the concept:

“Flowers Growing Out of an Object.”

As part of this creative brief, I was invited to contribute a 3-piece mini collection in my signature illustrative style. The artwork would be applied to products like greeting cards, gift bags, and envelope liners.

The key requirements:

  • A Hero Illustration showing flowers growing out of an object
  • A Secondary Floral Pattern
  • A Blender (Low-Volume) Pattern
  • All designs had to follow a cohesive color story (provided)
  • Dimensions: 8x8 inches, 300 DPI, CMYK

Color Story (provided by client)

I integrated the following hex values to ensure the work aligned with the overall collaborative collection:

  1. #e1ada1
  2. #e2724f
  3. #e7ac3b
  4. #178365
  5. #60ab96
  6. #b62f25
  7. #cddcd7
  8. #675627

Hero Illustration

Concept:

A desk lamp, traditionally a symbol of productivity and structure, is reimagined as a source of organic growth and creativity. Instead of light, a cascade of whimsical flowers bursts forth, flowing freely and softly illuminating the canvas with joy and charm.

Design Details:

  • Lamp base rendered in muted earthy tones for a grounded contrast
  • Bloom variety and flowing directionality mimic the natural movement of light rays
  • Delicate line work paired with textured shading for warmth and dimension
  • Used pops of warm coral and vibrant green from the palette to bring energy

Secondary Pattern

This repeating floral pattern features playful white petals arranged in a rhythmic grid on a deep green background. Each flower is centered with a pop of red, adding a vibrant contrast that catches the eye. The alternating orientation and petal styles give the layout a gentle visual movement, making it perfect for product applications like envelope liners, wrapping paper sides, or journal covers. It brings a sense of cheer and balance, echoing the organic flow from the hero illustration.

Blender Pattern

This low-volume pattern offers an abstract and elegant complement to the hero piece. Flowing, hand-drawn lines in gradient shades of gold and brown weave across a blush-pink background, mimicking the soft tangle of stems or organic roots. It creates a subtle texture without overpowering other designs—ideal for gift wrap backings, product borders, or interior accents. Minimalist and modern, it adds warmth and harmony to the collection.

Reflection

This project allowed me to blend my love for playful illustration and thoughtful surface design. The desk lamp illustration was especially meaningful—a metaphor for finding beauty and inspiration in the mundane. It was also a fun challenge to balance color vibrancy with usability across products.

The cohesive trio reflects my artistic style—whimsical, expressive, and grounded in nature—with enough versatility to be applied across various commercial product formats.
