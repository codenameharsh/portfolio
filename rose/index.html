My hardened heart grows a thorn.
A little prick
Can maim your soul.
Breathe out now,
My desperate whole.
Watch your feet,
Where they fall,
Follow your heart,
And bury your blood.
Poison bleeds
Through my rosy cheeks,
A dead man finds,
All that he seeks.
Forgiveness haunts
And glory weeps.
A sudden smile
Creeps into my dreams,
Sleep is lost,
And silence seeps.
Alone, at last
I cry at will.
My petals bloom
With each shuddered breath,
Beauty lies,
And I yearn to be held.
My sunshine burns,
My skin blood red.
I rose again from the land of the dead.
