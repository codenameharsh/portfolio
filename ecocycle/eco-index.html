Eco-Cycle: User Profile Design

Case Study | UI/UX Design

Role: UI Designer

Duration: 1.5 weeks

Project Overview

EcoCycle is a conceptual social platform centered around sustainable living, created as part of a Daily UI challenge. I designed a personalized user profile page that would act as a sustainability hub—something more than just a page of stats, but rather a reflection of a user’s eco-journey, values, and contributions to a greener world.

Why EcoCycle?

As someone deeply invested in designing intentional, impact-driven digital experiences, the idea of contributing to a platform that promotes climate action and community resonated deeply. I saw this challenge as a chance to explore how UI/UX design can drive positive behavior, build community, and make eco-conscious living feel accessible and rewarding.

Project Goals

From the start, I set out to design a user profile that was more than functional—it needed to inspire, inform, and empower.

    My Design Objectives:
  • Build a profile experience that celebrates a user’s sustainability efforts in a personalized and engaging way.
  • Encourage community connection and collective impact through social features and eco-challenges.
  • Maintain a clean, intuitive, and joyful interface aligned with EcoCycle’s green mission.
  • Apply sustainable design principles, ensuring visual harmony and thoughtful digital consumption.

Research & Learning Process

  1. Understanding the Audience

    To ground my design decisions in real user needs, I conducted a survey of 30 individuals with a strong interest in sustainability. These insights helped me frame both user pain points and emotional drivers.

      Key Takeaways:
    • 80% were motivated by climate change but lacked platforms to show their impact.
    • 60% craved community and connection around sustainability.
    • 55% wanted personalization—to reflect their unique journey.
    • 50% struggled to discover relevant, trustworthy eco-resources.

      • These insights told me something important: the user profile couldn’t just be a static dashboard. It had to be a dynamic, personal space that evolves with the user, connecting individual effort to a broader mission.

  2. Competitor Audit

    I evaluated 5 existing eco-apps, noting what worked and what didn’t:

    Platform Strengths Weaknesses
    EcoLife Strong community forum Limited personalization
    Greenify Gamification elements Weak community features
    EarthHero Educational resources Cluttered interface
    Sustainable Living Event calendar & tips Outdated design
    EcoWarrior Social sharing features Lacks a collective impact approach

    This audit validated three must-haves for my design:

    Key takeaways:

    • Gamified feedback for motivation
    • Community engagement
    • A visually cohesive, intuitive interface
  3. Affinity Mapping

    After analyzing the survey data,I created an affinity map to identify patterns and themes. This affinity map highlights the key themes and patterns that emerged from the survey data, including the importance of community features, personalized recommendations, and customization options.

  4. User Persona

    From the data emerged

    Emma, a 28-year-old marketing specialist and eco-warrior. Emma represents the many people who blend digital life with sustainability values—sharing, engaging, and always looking to do better.

    Designing for her meant keeping the interface lively, social, and meaningful.

  5. User Journey

    Based on the user persona I created, I designed a user journey map that follows Emma's journey from awareness, onboarding, exploration, connection, sharing and growth to acceptance.

Design Process

  1. Problem Definition

    Synthesizing the research, I arrived at a core question:
    "How might I design a personalized user profile for EcoCycle that empowers users to track, celebrate, and share their sustainability journey—while fostering a sense of community and purpose?"

    This guided every design decision that followed.

  2. Ideation

    I ran a solo design sprint using design thinking principles, breaking ideas into themes:

    • Gamification & Incentives
      • Eco-Badge System: To celebrate wins both big and small.
      • Sustainability Score: A dynamic visual that grows with consistent eco-action.
    • Personalization
      • Customizable widgets and themes: Users choose what to highlight.
      • Eco Goals: Set, track, and share personal milestones.
    • Community & Social Engagement
      • Shareable moments: Users can post eco-wins or badges on their feed or social media.
      • Monthly Community Challenges: Promoting a sense of shared purpose.
    • Information Discovery
    • AI-driven suggestions: AI-driven recommendations for eco-friendly tips.
    • Sustainability Leaderboard: Highlights top contributors in the community.

    From over 50 ideas, I refined the ones that directly aligned with Emma’s needs and the project’s scope.

  3. Wireframes & Prototypes
    • Wireframes (Low-Fidelity Designs)

      I started with rough sketches to clarify layout, then developed grayscale wireframes in Figma to establish hierarchy and interaction flow.

      Key Sections:

      • Profile Overview:

        Header with user's name and profile picture, Brief bio and eco-friendly interests, Eco-friendly achievements and badges, Call-to-action (CTA) button to edit profile.

      • Eco-Badge System Wireframe:

        Grid showcasing user's earned badges, Badge details, including description and date earned, CTA button to view all badges, Filter options to sort badges by category or date.

      • Customizable Profile Wireframe:

        Profile picture uploader with cropping tool, Cover photo uploader with resizing tool, Bio editor with formatting options, Eco-friendly interest selector with tags.

      • Social Sharing Wireframe:

        Share button with dropdown menu for social media platforms,Customizable share message with hashtags and tags, Preview of shared post with image and text, CTA button to share post

  4. Prototypes (High-Fidelity Designs)

    I developed interactive high-fidelity prototypes in Figma, incorporating refined UI elements:

    • UI Design Elements:
      • Nature-inspired Color Palette → Deep Green (#2E7D32), Earthy Beige (#F5F5DC),Sunflower yellow (#E4B646), Deep Red (#6D1111)
      • Typography → Modern sans-serif for readability, cursive accents for elegance.
      • Icons & Illustrations → Custom sustainability-themed icons for intuitive navigation.

      Each element was selected to reflect nature, authenticity, and emotional resonance.

Key Features

  • Personalized Sustainability Hub: Users can track eco-friendly achievements, update their interests, and visually represent their impact through badges and scores.
  • Eco-Badge System: Gamified system that rewards meaningful behaviors—like reducing plastic use, biking to work, or supporting ethical brands
  • Profile Customization: Editable bios, cover images, and interest tags empower users to tell their own sustainability story.
  • Social Sharing & Community Feed: Easily share milestones with the EcoCycle community or external platforms. Community challenges and leaderboards enhance engagement.
  • Discoverability Engine: Behind the scenes, AI curates content based on user activity and preferences, solving the problem of eco-content overload.

Final Outcomes & Impact

    The final prototype delivers a balanced mix of functionality, inspiration, and community spirit.
  • Engaging & Personalized User Profiles
    • Users now have a centralized hub to track their eco-friendly activities and engage with the community.
    • Customization features allow users to personalize their sustainability goals and display their impact visually.
  • Increased Community Engagement
    • The Eco-Badge System and Social Sharing features encourage users to actively participate in sustainability initiatives.
    • The Community Challenges feature fosters collective impact, strengthening the platform’s mission.
  • Enhanced User Experience & Visual Appeal
  • The clean UI, nature-inspired colors, and user-friendly navigation create an immersive experience.
  • Gamification elements keep users motivated without feeling overwhelming.
    • Even though it was built during a short sprint, the prototype lays strong groundwork for future expansion into a full-fledged platform.

Final Thoughts

Designing the EcoCycle User Profile was an insightful experience that strengthened my skills in UI/UX, gamification, and user-centered design. Through research, ideation, and iterative prototyping, I developed a functional, engaging, and aesthetically appealing solution that supports sustainable living and community building.

This project reaffirmed my passion for creating meaningful digital experiences that drive positive impact.
