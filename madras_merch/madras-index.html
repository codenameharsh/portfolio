Madras Merch: Celebrating Chennai Through Design

Case Study | Print-on-demand Designs

Duration: 2.5 weeks

Introduction

Madras Merch is a print-on-demand brand that pays tribute to Chennai’s rich culture, history, and everyday life through graphic design and illustration. The goal was to develop a unique collection of 10 merchandise designs that capture the essence of the city—its architecture, language, art, and passion for cinema, cricket, and food.

I was responsible for crafting culturally resonant, visually compelling designs while ensuring print compatibility across various products.

Project Goals

  • Create a collection of 10 unique, high-resolution print designs that reflect Chennai’s culture.
  • Ensure consistency in branding through color, typography, and design principles.
  • Develop scalable, print-ready vector artwork adaptable for t-shirts, posters, and merchandise.

Research & Concept Development

Before diving into the designs, I researched Chennai’s cultural identity to select key themes that resonate with locals and those connected to the city.

  1. Identifying Key Cultural Elements
      To ensure authenticity, I explored:
    • Landmarks: Chennai Central, Marina Beach.
    • Street Life: Autos, street signs, typography.
    • Food Culture: Filter Coffee, Idli-Vada-Sambar.
    • Traditional Arts: Bharatanatyam
    • Sports & Cinema: CSK’s cricket legacy, Tamil cinema references
  2. Mood Board & Visual Direction
      I curated a mood board featuring:
    • Vintage Tamil poster art & street signage for bold, expressive typography.
    • Muted yet vibrant color palettes found in Chennai’s architecture and markets.
    • Elements of hand-drawn textures and clean vector styles for a mix of nostalgia and modernity.

Design Process

The 10 Thematic Merchandise Designs

Each design is inspired by Chennai’s cultural richness, blending illustration, typography, and storytelling:

  • Madras Filter Kaapi: A detailed exploded diagram of the traditional South Indian coffee filter, showcasing its four-layered structure. The design features hand-drawn annotations in Tamil & English, celebrating Chennai’s love for strong, aromatic filter kaapi.
  • The Auto Rickshaw: A bold, stylized illustration of Chennai’s yellow auto, featuring local slang typography: "Meter-ku mela oru 20 roobah…” (An iconic phrase referring to extra fare charged by auto drivers). This design captures the city's chaotic, vibrant street life with hand-drawn textures and motion effects.
  • Bharatanatyam: A graceful depiction of a classical Bharatanatyam dancer, capturing the essence of Mudras (hand gestures) and traditional jewelry. The illustration is designed with flowing lines, intricate detailing, and a deep red-gold color palette, emphasizing Chennai’s classical heritage.
  • Namma Chennai Central: A detailed line-art illustration of the Chennai Central Railway Station, one of the most recognizable heritage landmarks. The vintage poster aesthetic, combined with bold Tamil typography, gives this piece a timeless and nostalgic feel.
  • CSK Whistle Podu: Inspired by Chennai Super Kings’ (CSK) cricket fandom, this design captures the energy of the "Whistle Podu" movement.
  • CSK Batsman Silhouette: A high-energy, action-packed sports illustration, showcasing a Chennai Super Kings batsman mid-swing.
  • Idli Vada Sambar: A fun, minimalist food illustration featuring a classic Tamil breakfast: steaming idlis, crispy vadas, and hot sambar served on a banana leaf. The design is clean, modern, and instantly recognizable to any Chennaiite.
  • Madras Presidency Map: A vintage-style historical map, featuring the original boundaries of the Madras Presidency (a colonial-era region that included Chennai). The aged-paper effect and intricate line detailing make this a collector’s item for history enthusiasts.

Final Outcomes & Impact

  • Cohesive Brand Identity & Print-Ready Designs
    • The collection successfully captured Chennai’s cultural richness while maintaining a modern, stylish appeal.
    • Designs were optimized for various merchandise formats, ensuring versatility across t-shirts, posters, and accessories.
  • Strong Cultural Connection & Market Appeal
    • The artwork resonated with Chennaiites, Tamil diaspora, and culture enthusiasts.
    • The balance of regional nostalgia with contemporary design aesthetics made the collection visually engaging and commercially viable.
>

Final Thoughts

Designing for Madras Merch was an exciting opportunity to merge culture, nostalgia, and modern aesthetics into commercially viable artwork. The project deepened my understanding of:

  • Regional branding & visual storytelling.
  • Typography and illustration-driven merchandise design.
  • Print production workflows & scalability considerations.

Through thoughtful research, illustration, and typography, I was able to craft a collection that resonates with Chennai’s cultural identity while remaining market-friendly and visually compelling.
