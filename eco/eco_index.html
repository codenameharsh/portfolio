Case Study:

Eco-Cycle UI/UX

UI Design UX Research User Profile Screen Design

PROJECT OVERVIEW

EcoCycle is a conceptual social platform designed to transform the standard user profile into a personalized sustainability hub. Developed as part of a Daily UI challenge, the project focuses on reflecting a user’s unique eco-journey, values, and community contributions.

  • Role: UI Designer
  • Duration: 1.5 weeks

DESIGN PILLARS


01

Personalization

Celebrating individual sustainability efforts through an engaging, tailored experience

02

Community

Driving collective impact by encouraging social connections and participation in shared eco-challenges

03

Clarity & Joy

Balancing global high-fashion standards with the operational realities of a fast-paced retail environment.

04

Sustainability

Applying sustainable design principles to ensure visual harmony and thoughtful digital consumption.

VISUAL DNA

  • Color Palette: A nature-inspired selection featuring Deep Green (#2E7D32), Earthy Beige (#F5F5DC), Sunflower Yellow (#E4B646), and Deep Red (#6D1111).
  • Typography: Modern sans-serif for readability paired with cursive accents for a touch of elegance.
  • Iconography: Custom sustainability-themed icons designed for intuitive navigation.

TECH STACK

  • Design & Prototyping: Figma (used for grayscale wireframes and high-fidelity interactive prototypes).
  • Research Tools: Surveys (30 participants) and Competitor Audits (5 platforms).

Challenge

Research indicated that while 80% of users are motivated by climate change, 50% struggle to find relevant information and 60% crave better community connection.

THE PROBLEM

"How might I design a personalized user profile for EcoCycle that empowers users to track, celebrate, and share their sustainability journey while fostering a sense of community and purpose?"

key features

CORE COMPONENTS & LOGIC

01

Personalized Sustainability Hub

A centralized space where users track achievements, update interests, and view their impact via scores.

02

Eco-Badge System

A gamified reward system for behaviors like reducing plastic, biking to work, or supporting ethical brands.

03

Profile Customization

Empowerment through editable bios, cover images, and interest tags to tell a personal story.

04

Social Sharing & Feed

Tools to share milestones with the EcoCycle community or external social platforms.

05

Discoverability Engine

A curation tool that provides personalized eco-friendly tips and content.

DESIGN PROCESS

The project followed the Norman Nielsen Group (NN/g) design model, a user-centered approach:

  1. Research: Surveys and competitor audits to identify pain points.
  2. Personas: Creating "Emma," a 28-year-old eco-warrior, to ground design decisions.
  3. Ideation: Solo design sprints generating over 50 ideas categorized into themes like gamification and social engagement.
  4. Wireframing: Grayscale layouts in Figma to establish information hierarchy.
  5. Prototyping: High-fidelity interactive designs incorporating the final "Visual DNA".

The Refinement Arc

The Pivot:
Realizing through research that the profile could not be a static dashboard; it had to be a dynamic, evolving space that connected individual efforts to a broader mission.

Prioritization:
Using a combination of user needs, business goals, and technical feasibility to narrow down 50 ideas to the top 3 features: the Eco-badge system, customizable profiles, and social sharing.

CHALLENGES AND TAKEAWAYS

THE HURDLES

  • Synthesizing Fragmented Data
    My research showed that while interest in sustainability is high, users felt overwhelmed by "eco-content overload."
    The challenge was designing a profile that acts as a filter rather than adding to the noise, balancing a data-rich dashboard with a clean, joyful interface.
  • The Gamification Tightrope
    I had to carefully calibrate the Eco-Badge and Leaderboard systems. The goal was to create a sense of accomplishment without making sustainability feel like a high-pressure competition that could lead to user burnout or "eco-guilt."
  • Defining "Impact" Visually
    One of the toughest hurdles was moving beyond static numbers. I had to figure out how to visually represent a "sustainability journey" that felt personal and evolving, rather than just a dry collection of carbon offset statistics.

THE INSIGHTS

  • Connection is the Core Metric
    I learned that for 40% of my target audience, the profile isn't just about self-reflection—it’s a social bridge. A successful UI must treat community connection as a primary utility, not just a secondary feature.
  • Customization Drives Ownership
    Seeing that 55% of users prioritized customization taught me that sustainability is deeply personal. By allowing users to choose their own themes and interest tags, I gave them a sense of ownership over their environmental narrative.
  • Behavioral Design over Documentation
    I realized that users are more motivated by seeing their growth over time than by looking at historical data. This shifted my focus toward "journey-based" design, prioritizing milestone celebrations and badges.

Scope & Future Improvements

  • Precision AI Recommendations
    I plan to refine the discovery engine to provide hyper-localized, trustworthy eco-resources based on specific user activity and geographic needs.
  • Scaling the Ecosystem
    Based on the 80% of survey participants who requested more social tools, my next step is to expand the profile into a full ecosystem featuring community discussion boards, local event calendars, and collaborative challenge hubs.
