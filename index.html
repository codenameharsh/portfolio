About Me

Hey, I’m Harshitha, a visual storyteller, creative thinker, and all-around design enthusiast. I blend graphic design, branding, and UI/UX to create digital experiences that are not only beautiful but meaningful. My journey started at the intersection of fashion and art, where I realized how much I love bringing ideas to life visually, emotionally, and sometimes, even with a bit of code.


Since then, I’ve had the chance to work with some amazing brands and startups: designing logos, building brand identities, illustrating infographics, putting together lookbooks, and crafting interactive user interfaces. Along the way, I got curious about what happens behind the screen too. That curiosity led me into front-end development and full-stack JavaScript , through platforms like Meta, IBM (Coursera), FreeCodeCamp, The Odin Project, and DailyUI. I’m currently pursuing the Google UX Design Certification to strengthen my foundation in user-centered design, to kickstart a successful career transition into the world of UI/UX.

My Creative Journey:

  • 2017 – Started in Fashion & Design
    • Studied Fashion Management, Digital Design, and Business Marketing
    • Explored storytelling through visual branding and editorial design
  • 2019–2021 – Branded Visual Experiences
    • Designed logos, lookbooks, and brand identities
    • Worked on infographics and promotional materials for small businesses and startups
  • 2022 – Transitioned into UI/UX & Development
    • Learned user-centered design and interaction principles
    • Began exploring front-end development and full-stack JavaScript through:
      • Meta & IBM (Coursera)
      • FreeCodeCamp
      • The Odin Project
      • Daily UI Challenges
  • 2025 (Now) – Focused on UX & Career Growth
    • Pursuing Google UX Design Certification
    • Passionate about crafting intentional, user-first digital experiences

Outside of design, I’m probably nose-deep in a book, lost in a painting, scribbling poetry, hunting down the spiciest food on the menu, or re-reading Harry Potter for the hundredth time. ("After all this time?" "Always.")
