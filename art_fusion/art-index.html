ArtFusion: UI Design

Case Study | 3-Step Sign-Up Page for an Art Showcasing Event

Duration: 4 days

Project Overview

ArtFusion is an annual art showcasing event that celebrates local artists, art students, and creative communities. I took on this project as an opportunity to go beyond a basic sign-up page prompt and design a more holistic experience—one that felt inclusive, expressive, and thoughtfully crafted for a creative audience.

The challenge was to balance usability and aesthetics—making the process inviting for artists of all backgrounds, while also ensuring the user experience was smooth across mobile, tablet, and desktop devices.The final design is a responsive, 3-step sign-up flow that balances visual clarity with creative warmth, aimed at making artists feel welcomed, understood, and excited to participate.

Project Goals

This project started as a Daily UI challenge, but I decided to treat it like a real-world design problem:

  • Design a user-friendly sign-up flow tailored to the needs of artists submitting their work.
  • Collect meaningful data for event organizers in a structured, non-intimidating way.
  • Create a responsive experience that works effortlessly on mobile, tablet, and desktop.
  • Encourage post-submission engagement, with an emphasis on social sharing and community visibility.

Research & Learning Process

Before jumping into the wireframes, I paused to reflect on the audience:

  1. Artists are often visual thinkers who appreciate intuitive interfaces but dislike friction in tech experiences.
  2. Many may be applying to showcase for the first time, so the process needed to feel welcoming, not transactional.
  3. I looked into real-world submission forms from art fairs and portfolio platforms and noted common issues: cluttered layouts, overwhelming fields, and a lack of clarity around next steps.

I also revisited UX principles around progressive disclosure, mobile-first design, and form psychology—particularly how to build trust and reduce cognitive load throughout multi-step interactions.

Design Process

    Problem Definition
  • How can we make the sign-up experience feel less like “filling out a form” and more like joining a creative movement?
    Ideation

    I wanted the interaction to mirror the emotional journey of participating in an art show—from curiosity, to commitment, to celebration. I envisioned a flow that started with an inspiring introduction, followed by a focused form, and ended on an uplifting note with encouragement to share the news.

    Key ideas that guided the UX:

  • One step at a time to avoid overwhelming users
  • Clear visual hierarchy for fast scanning
  • Warm, supportive tone in microcopy
  • Subtle design flourishes that nod to the artistic theme
    Wireframes & Prototypes

    Using Figma, I sketched and prototyped the full flow:

  • Landing Page: A bold hero section introducing ArtFusion, benefits of participating, and a prominent CTA to “Submit Your Work.”
  • 3-Step Form Flow:
    • Artist Info – Basic contact details
    • Artwork Submission – Title, medium, dimensions, image upload
    • Statement & Notes – Artist statement + optional message to organizers
  • Confirmation Page: A feel-good success screen with options to download a PDF receipt and share the event on social media

    • I kept visual distractions minimal—favoring whitespace, clean typography, and intentional color accents to keep the focus on the task while still feeling visually polished.

User Flow

  1. Landing Page:
    • Quick introduction to ArtFusion
    • Hero image with clear CTA
    • Highlights of artist benefits
    • Footer with social links and contact info
  2. 3-Step Sign-Up Form:
    • Step 1: Artist Info
      • Name
      • Email
      • Phone Number
    • Step 2: Artwork Details
      • Title
      • Medium
      • Dimensions
      • Image Upload
    • Step 3: Submission Requirements
      • Artist Statement
      • Additional Notes
      • CTA to Submit

Key Features

  1. Step-by-Step Sign-Up

    Dividing the form into digestible stages reduced overwhelm and boosted completion confidence.

  2. Responsive UI

    Carefully adapted layouts for phone, tablet, and desktop—ensuring the form remains clear and accessible no matter how it’s accessed.

  3. Clean, Minimalist Layout

    Focused on clarity and ease-of-use, with whitespace and alignment to reduce cognitive friction.

  4. Artist-Centric Microcopy

    Friendly, human language that aligns with the creative tone of the event—less “submit form,” more “show your art to the world.”

  5. Post-Submission Engagement

    A subtle CTA to share their submission on social media, along with downloadable confirmation, added a sense of accomplishment and social momentum.

Tools Used

  • Figma: UI design + prototyping
  • Illustrator: For icons and vector elements
  • Photoshop: Image prep

Final Outcome & Impact

The final product is a responsive, artist-first sign-up experience that feels as thoughtful and expressive as the event it supports. It doesn’t just collect data—it invites participation.

By thinking through the emotional and practical needs of ArtFusion’s community, I was able to create a sign-up journey that:

  • Feels personal and artist-friendly
  • Looks clean and professional on all screen sizes
  • Encourages event visibility through built-in social prompts
  • Demonstrates UX empathy, technical skill, and responsive design in action

This project also gave me the opportunity to explore design as an invitation, to make the act of submitting feel like joining something meaningful.

Final Thoughts

    Key Takeaways
  • Great design is about empathy. Designing for artists meant shifting from transactional UI to expressive, story-driven UX.
  • Progressive disclosure works. Breaking complex forms into stages improved clarity and reduced drop-off.
  • Details build trust. Thoughtful microcopy, visual consistency, and smooth transitions all contributed to a positive experience.
    Future Improvements
  • Add a dashboard for artists to manage/edit submissions
  • Allow multi-artwork uploads for more diverse submissions
  • Include automated email confirmations or event reminders
  • Integrate a social media preview to make post-submission sharing more seamless
